Internal Auditing Chart & Operations

Internal Audit

FEDS’s internal audit is an independent unit directly under the Board of Directors. Besides the Board of Trustees regular meeting reports, and monthly or when necessary, to the Chairman, the supervisors, and the president of the report.

The Auditing Office is responsible for investigating and evaluating the shortcomings of the internal control system and timely providing recommendations for improvement to ensure the internal control system can be effectively and continuously implemented, and assist the Board of Directors and the management fulfills its responsibilities truly; its scope to bear with the company and its subsidiaries are open for review by internal auditor.

In accordance with the article of the “Regulations Governing Establishment of Internal Control Systems by Public Companies” promulgated by the Securities and Futures Bureau of Financial Supervisory Commission of the Executive Yuan, the auditing office formulates its annual auditing plan. Base on identified risks, the auditing work is executed on regular or irregular intervals in order to really assess the internal control system to comply with the integrity, rationality and effectiveness.

The company supplements the review activities by internal audit with documented self- inspection by the departments and operational units. Internal Audit sees to it that these activities do take place, and reviews the submissions to ensure the quality of the process; it consolidates the results of the self- inspection for the president and the Board of Directors.
